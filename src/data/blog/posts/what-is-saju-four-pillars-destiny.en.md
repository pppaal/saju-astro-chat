## Introduction to Saju (Four Pillars of Destiny)

Saju (사주, 四柱), also known as the Four Pillars of Destiny, is an East Asian system of fate analysis with roots in the Tang dynasty and a structure formalized during the Song dynasty (around the 10th century) by the scholar Xu Ziping (徐子平), who added the hour pillar and shifted the analytical focus to the Day Master. It has been studied and practiced in Korea for roughly a thousand years. The term "Saju" literally means "four pillars," referring to the four components of your birth moment: year, month, day, and hour. Because each pillar contains two characters (a Heavenly Stem and an Earthly Branch), the full chart is also called "Sajupalja" (사주팔자, 四柱八字) — "four pillars, eight characters."

## The Philosophy Behind Saju

At the core of Saju lie two layered concepts: Yin-Yang (음양, 陰陽) — the complementary polarities of every force — and the Five Elements (오행, 五行): Wood (木), Fire (火), Earth (土), Metal (金), and Water (水). The elements relate through a generating cycle (상생, e.g. Wood feeds Fire) and a controlling cycle (상극, e.g. Water extinguishes Fire), and each element carries either a yin or yang charge.

Each person's birth chart contains a unique combination of these elements, expressed through the 10 Heavenly Stems (천간, 天干) and 12 Earthly Branches (지지, 地支). The pairing of one stem with one branch generates the 60-year sexagenary cycle (육십갑자, 六十甲子) that underlies the calendar. The balance and interaction of these elements in your chart shape your:

- **Personality traits** and natural tendencies
- **Career aptitudes** and professional success
- **Relationship compatibility** with others
- **Health patterns** and vulnerabilities
- **Fortune cycles** throughout your life

## The Four Pillars Explained

### Year Pillar (年柱, Yeonju)

Represents your ancestral influences, social position, and early childhood environment. It shows how the world perceives you and your relationship with society.

### Month Pillar (月柱, Wolju)

Reflects your parents' influence, career path, and young adult life. This pillar often indicates your primary profession and public achievements.

### Day Pillar (日柱, Ilju)

The most important pillar — it represents your core self, your spouse, and your adult life. The upper character of this pillar is the Day Master (일간, 日干, Ilgan), the single Heavenly Stem that stands for "you" and serves as the reference point all other elements are weighed against. The lower character (일지, 日支) is traditionally read as the spouse's palace.

### Hour Pillar (時柱, Siju)

Represents your children, creativity, later years, and legacy. It shows what you leave behind for future generations.

## How Saju Differs from Western Astrology

While both systems analyze birth time, Saju and Western astrology have key differences. A common misconception is that Saju runs on the lunar calendar — it actually uses the 24 solar terms (절기, 節氣) tracked by a perpetual calendar called the manseryeok (만세력). The Saju year, for example, doesn't start on lunar New Year but at Ipchun (입춘, around February 4), and each monthly pillar changes at a specific solar term, not at a new moon.

| Saju                                       | Western Astrology                       |
| ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| Uses the 24 solar terms (manseryeok)       | Uses tropical/sidereal zodiac signs     |
| Built on Yin-Yang and the Five Elements    | Built on planetary positions and houses |
| 60-year stem-and-branch cycle (육십갑자)   | 12 zodiac signs across the year         |
| Focuses on elemental balance and timing    | Focuses on planetary aspects            |

## Understanding Your Saju Reading

A professional Saju reading analyzes:

1. **Day Master strength**: Is your core element strong or weak in this chart?
2. **Elemental balance**: Which elements are excessive, deficient, or missing?
3. **Useful god (용신, 用神)**: The element that best brings the chart into balance — your strategic ally.
4. **Ten Gods (십신, 十神)**: Ten relational roles (such as 비견 Friend, 식신 Eating God, 정재 Direct Wealth, 정관 Direct Officer, 정인 Direct Resource) that each remaining stem and branch plays in relation to the Day Master. They translate raw elements into themes like career, wealth, recognition, relationships, and learning.
5. **Luck pillars (대운, 大運)**: 10-year periods that overlay new stems and branches onto the natal chart.
6. **Annual fortune (세운, 歲運)**: The current year's stem and branch and how they interact with the chart.

## Practical Applications

Saju is used in Korea for:

- **Naming children**: Choosing characters that balance the child's chart
- **Marriage compatibility**: Analyzing if couples are harmonious
- **Business decisions**: Timing important ventures
- **Career guidance**: Finding suitable professions
- **Health management**: Understanding vulnerabilities

## Conclusion

Saju offers a structured lens on your life's blueprint. Classical practitioners are clear that the chart is not a script: it describes the field of energies you were born into, while choices, environment, and effort decide how that field plays out. Understanding your Saju is less about being told what will happen and more about recognizing your raw materials — the elements you have a lot of, the ones you lack, and the timing of the seasons ahead.
